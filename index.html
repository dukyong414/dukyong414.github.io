About Me

Hello everyone.
My name is Dukyong Kim, and I am currently pursuing a Ph.D. in Mechanical Engineering at Northwestern University’s Evanston campus. I was born and raised in Seoul, South Korea, where I earned both my B.S. and M.S. degrees from Hanyang University. As a passionate enthusiast of automobiles—from Hot Wheels to Formula One—I’ve long been fascinated by the mechanical behavior of cars. This curiosity led me to delve deeply into mechanical engineering, where I became captivated by the multifunctionality of lasers in manufacturing—not only in the automotive industry but also across advanced sectors. During my undergraduate studies, I focused on laser-based Additive Manufacturing (AM) processes for my senior research project. Building on that foundation, I expanded my expertise into pulsed laser testing and interfacial adhesion analysis for advanced packaging. As I advance through my Ph.D. journey at Northwestern, I aim to collaborate with distinguished researchers and leverage cutting-edge experimental facilities to deepen my understanding of laser-based manufacturing. My goal is to identify and mitigate defect formation mechanisms, ultimately contributing to the global advancement and reliability of next-generation manufacturing technologies.

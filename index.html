About Me

Hello everyone.
My name is Dukyong Kim, and I am currently pursuing a Ph.D. in Mechanical Engineering at Northwestern University’s Evanston campus. I was born and raised in Seoul, South Korea, where I earned both my B.S. and M.S. degrees from Hanyang University. As a passionate enthusiast of automobiles—from Hot Wheels to Formula One—I’ve long been fascinated by the mechanical behavior of cars. This curiosity led me to delve deeply into mechanical engineering, where I became captivated by the multifunctionality of lasers in manufacturing—not only in the automotive industry but also across aerospace, shipbuilding, and semiconductor sectors. During my undergraduate studies, I focused on laser-based Additive Manufacturing (AM) processes for my senior research project. Building on that foundation, I expanded my expertise into pulsed laser-induced shock wave testing, commonly known as the Laser Spallation Test, which is highly effective for quantifying the interfacial adhesion properties of thin films As I advance through my Ph.D. journey at Northwestern, I aim to collaborate with distinguished researchers and leverage cutting-edge experimental facilities to deepen my understanding of laser–material interactions during AM processes. My goal is to identify and mitigate defect formation mechanisms, ultimately contributing to the global advancement and reliability of next-generation manufacturing technologies.
